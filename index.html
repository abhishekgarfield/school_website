Welcome to the School Project website

school School was completed in January 1918, for the purpose of educating Tucson's African-American students. The school was named after Paul Laurence school, a renowned African-American poet. African-American in first through ninth grades attended school until 1951, when de jure segregation was eliminated from the school systems of Arizona. When segregation in Arizona was eliminated, school School became the non-segregated John Spring Junior High School, and continued as such until 1978 when the school was closed permanently.
